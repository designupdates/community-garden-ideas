Garden Furniture Cushion Storage: Keeping Your Cushions Safe and Secure

Written by Adams Aug 14, 2025 Â· 10 min read

Garden furniture cushions are a great way to add comfort and style to your outdoor living space. However, when the weather turns bad or you're not using them, you need to find a way to store them safely and securely.

In this article, we'll provide you with some tips for storing your garden furniture cushions properly. We'll also discuss some of the different storage options available, so you can choose the one that's best for your needs.

By following these tips, you can keep your garden furniture cushions looking their best for years to come.

garden furniture cushion storage

Protect cushions from weather damage.

  • Clean cushions before storing.
  • Dry cushions thoroughly.
  • Store cushions in a cool, dry place.
  • Use protective storage containers.
  • Inspect cushions regularly.

By following these tips, you can keep your garden furniture cushions looking their best for years to come.

Clean cushions before storing.

Before you store your garden furniture cushions, it's important to clean them thoroughly. This will help to remove any dirt, pollen, or debris that could attract pests or cause the cushions to mildew. It will also help to keep your cushions looking their best for longer.

To clean your garden furniture cushions, you can use a mild detergent and a soft brush. Be sure to rinse the cushions thoroughly with water afterwards. If the cushions are heavily soiled, you may need to soak them in a solution of mild detergent and water for a few hours before cleaning them.

Once the cushions are clean, allow them to dry thoroughly before storing them. You can do this by hanging them in a shady spot or by laying them flat on a clean surface. Make sure the cushions are completely dry before storing them, as any moisture can cause mildew to grow.

Once the cushions are clean and dry, you can store them in a cool, dry place. A garage, shed, or basement are all good options. If you don't have a suitable storage space, you can also use a storage container. Just be sure that the container is waterproof and airtight to protect the cushions from moisture and pests.

By following these tips, you can keep your garden furniture cushions clean and protected while they're in storage.

Dry cushions thoroughly.

Once you have cleaned your garden furniture cushions, it's important to dry them thoroughly before storing them. This will help to prevent mildew and mold from growing on the cushions, which can cause them to deteriorate and become unusable.

  • Hang cushions to dry:

    One of the best ways to dry your cushions is to hang them in a shady spot. This will allow the air to circulate all around the cushions, helping them to dry evenly. If you don't have a suitable place to hang your cushions, you can also lay them flat on a clean surface.

  • Use a fan:

    If you're in a hurry to dry your cushions, you can use a fan to help circulate the air. Just be sure to place the fan so that it's not blowing directly on the cushions, as this could cause them to fade.

  • Use a dehumidifier:

    If you live in a humid climate, you may need to use a dehumidifier to help dry your cushions. A dehumidifier will remove moisture from the air, helping to prevent mildew and mold from growing on the cushions.

  • Check cushions for dryness:

    Before you store your cushions, be sure to check them to make sure they are completely dry. If the cushions are still damp, they could develop mildew or mold while they're in storage.

By following these tips, you can dry your garden furniture cushions thoroughly and prevent them from mildewing or molding while they're in storage.

Store cushions in a cool, dry place.

Once your garden furniture cushions are clean and dry, it's important to store them in a cool, dry place. This will help to protect them from moisture, pests, and fading.

A garage, shed, or basement are all good options for storing garden furniture cushions. If you don't have a suitable storage space, you can also use a storage container. Just be sure that the container is waterproof and airtight to protect the cushions from moisture and pests.

When storing your cushions, be sure to place them in a single layer. This will help to prevent them from becoming compressed and misshapen. You should also avoid storing cushions in direct sunlight, as this can cause them to fade.

If you live in a humid climate, you may want to consider using a dehumidifier in your storage space. This will help to remove moisture from the air and prevent mildew and mold from growing on the cushions.

By following these tips, you can store your garden furniture cushions properly and protect them from damage.

Use protective storage containers.

If you don't have a suitable storage space for your garden furniture cushions, you can use protective storage containers to protect them from moisture, pests, and fading.

When choosing a storage container for your cushions, be sure to choose one that is the right size and shape. The container should be large enough to fit all of your cushions comfortably, but it shouldn't be so large that the cushions are loose and can move around inside the container.

You should also choose a storage container that is made from a durable material, such as plastic or metal. The container should also be waterproof and airtight to protect the cushions from moisture and pests.

Once you have chosen a suitable storage container, be sure to clean it thoroughly before using it. This will help to prevent pests from being attracted to the cushions.

By using protective storage containers, you can keep your garden furniture cushions safe and protected while they're in storage.

Inspect cushions regularly.

Once you have stored your garden furniture cushions, it's important to inspect them regularly to make sure they are in good condition. This will help you to identify any problems early on, before they can cause serious damage to the cushions.

When inspecting your cushions, be sure to check for the following:

  • Mold and mildew: Mold and mildew can grow on cushions if they are stored in a damp environment. If you see any signs of mold or mildew, clean the cushions with a mild detergent and water. You may also need to use a dehumidifier to remove moisture from the storage area.
Pests: Pests, such as mice and insects, can damage cushions by chewing on them or nesting in them. If you see any signs of pests, such as droppings or holes in the cushions, take steps to eliminate the pests from the storage area.Fading: Cushions can fade if they are stored in direct sunlight. If you are storing your cushions in a sunny location, be sure to cover them with a tarp or other protective covering.Tears and rips: Cushions can tear or rip if they are not handled properly. Be careful when moving and storing your cushions to avoid damaging them.

By inspecting your cushions regularly, you can keep them in good condition and extend their lifespan.
