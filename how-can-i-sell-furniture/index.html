How to Sell Furniture: A Comprehensive Guide to Getting Rid of Your Old Furniture

Written by William Apr 20, 2025 Â· 16 min read

Selling furniture can be a great way to declutter your home, make some extra money, and give your old furniture a new life. Whether you're selling furniture online, through a consignment shop, or at a garage sale, there are a few things you can do to make the process as easy and successful as possible.

In this comprehensive guide, we'll walk you through everything you need to know about selling furniture, from preparing your furniture for sale to pricing it competitively and marketing it effectively. We'll also provide tips for selling furniture online, through a consignment shop, and at a garage sale.

To prepare your furniture for sale, you'll need to clean it thoroughly, make any necessary repairs, and stage it attractively. Once your furniture is ready to sell, you'll need to price it competitively. You can do this by researching the market value of similar items online or by asking for a professional appraisal. Finally, you'll need to market your furniture effectively to reach potential buyers.

  • Clean and repair furniture
  • Stage furniture attractively
  • Research market value
  • Price furniture competitively
  • Take high-quality photos
  • Write detailed descriptions
  • Market furniture effectively
  • Negotiate with buyers

By following these steps, you can increase your chances of selling your furniture quickly and for a fair price.

Clean and repair furniture

Before you sell your furniture, it's important to clean and repair it to make it as appealing as possible to potential buyers. Here are some tips:

Clean furniture thoroughly. This means more than just dusting and vacuuming. You'll need to clean all surfaces of the furniture, including the upholstery, wood, and metal. Use a mild detergent and warm water, and be sure to rinse the furniture thoroughly to remove any soap residue. Dry the furniture completely with a clean cloth.

Make repairs as needed. If your furniture has any damage, such as scratches, dents, or tears, you'll need to repair it before you sell it. This will help to improve the appearance of the furniture and make it more valuable to potential buyers. You can make some repairs yourself, such as tightening screws or filling in scratches with wood filler. For more extensive repairs, you may need to hire a professional.

Reupholster furniture if necessary. If the upholstery on your furniture is worn or damaged, you may want to consider reupholstering it. This is a great way to give your furniture a fresh new look and make it more appealing to potential buyers. You can reupholster furniture yourself if you have the skills and tools, or you can hire a professional upholsterer.

Stage furniture attractively. Once you've cleaned and repaired your furniture, you need to stage it attractively to make it more appealing to potential buyers. This means arranging the furniture in a way that shows off its best features. You may also want to add some decorative pillows, throws, or plants to help stage the furniture.

By following these tips, you can clean and repair your furniture to make it more appealing to potential buyers and increase your chances of selling it quickly and for a fair price.

Stage furniture attractively

Once you've cleaned and repaired your furniture, you need to stage it attractively to make it more appealing to potential buyers. This means arranging the furniture in a way that shows off its best features. You may also want to add some decorative pillows, throws, or plants to help stage the furniture.

  • Arrange furniture in a functional and visually appealing way. When arranging your furniture, think about how people will move around the room and how they will use the furniture. You want to create a layout that is both functional and visually appealing. For example, you might place a sofa and chairs in a conversation area, and a dining table and chairs in a dining area.
  • Highlight the best features of your furniture. When staging your furniture, make sure to highlight its best features. For example, if you have a sofa with a beautiful upholstery pattern, you might place it in a prominent location in the room. Or, if you have a dining table with a unique design, you might use a tablecloth that complements the table's design.
  • Use decorative pillows and throws to add color and texture. Decorative pillows and throws can be a great way to add color and texture to your furniture. When choosing pillows and throws, select colors and patterns that complement the colors and patterns of your furniture. You can also use pillows and throws to create a specific mood or style in the room.
  • Add plants to add life and freshness. Plants can add life and freshness to any room. When choosing plants for your furniture, select plants that are easy to care for and that will complement the style of your furniture. You might place a potted plant on a side table or a coffee table, or you might hang a plant from the ceiling.

By following these tips, you can stage your furniture attractively to make it more appealing to potential buyers and increase your chances of selling it quickly and for a fair price.

Research market value

Before you price your furniture, you need to research the market value of similar items. This will help you to ensure that you're pricing your furniture competitively.

  • Look for similar items online. One of the best ways to research the market value of your furniture is to look for similar items online. You can use websites like eBay, Craigslist, and Facebook Marketplace to find items that are similar to yours. Pay attention to the prices of these items and use them to get an idea of what your furniture is worth.
  • Visit local furniture stores. Another way to research the market value of your furniture is to visit local furniture stores. Look at the prices of similar items in the stores and use them to get an idea of what your furniture is worth. You can also ask the sales staff about the current market value of your furniture.
  • Get a professional appraisal. If you're not sure how to price your furniture, you can get a professional appraisal. A professional appraiser will assess the condition, age, and style of your furniture and give you an estimate of its value. This can be a helpful way to get an accurate idea of what your furniture is worth.
  • Consider the condition of your furniture. When researching the market value of your furniture, be sure to consider its condition. Furniture that is in good condition will sell for more than furniture that is in poor condition. If your furniture has any damage, such as scratches, dents, or tears, you will need to factor this into the price.

By following these tips, you can research the market value of your furniture and price it competitively. This will help you to sell your furniture quickly and for a fair price.

Price furniture competitively

Once you know the market value of your furniture, you need to price it competitively. This means pricing your furniture at a price that is in line with the prices of similar items. If you price your furniture too high, potential buyers will be less likely to purchase it. If you price your furniture too low, you will lose out on potential profit.

  • Consider the market value of your furniture. When pricing your furniture, be sure to consider the market value of similar items. You can use the tips in the previous section to research the market value of your furniture.
  • Consider the condition of your furniture. The condition of your furniture will also affect its price. Furniture that is in good condition will sell for more than furniture that is in poor condition. If your furniture has any damage, such as scratches, dents, or tears, you will need to factor this into the price.
  • Consider the age of your furniture. The age of your furniture can also affect its price. Antique furniture and vintage furniture can sell for more than newer furniture. However, the age of your furniture is not always a determining factor in its price. Some newer furniture styles can also sell for high prices.
  • Consider the demand for your furniture. The demand for your furniture will also affect its price. If there is a high demand for your furniture, you will be able to sell it for a higher price. You can research the demand for your furniture by looking at online listings and talking to local furniture stores.

By following these tips, you can price your furniture competitively and increase your chances of selling it quickly and for a fair price.

Take high-quality photos

High-quality photos are essential for selling furniture online or through a consignment shop. Good photos will help to showcase your furniture's best features and make it more appealing to potential buyers. Here are some tips for taking high-quality photos of your furniture:

Use a good camera. If you have a DSLR camera, that's great! But even if you only have a smartphone, you can still take good photos of your furniture. Just make sure that your camera is clean and that the lens is free of smudges or fingerprints.

Choose a well-lit area. Natural light is best for taking photos of furniture. If you can, take your photos outdoors on a cloudy day. If you have to take photos indoors, use a room with plenty of windows. Avoid using flash, as this can create harsh shadows.

Stage your furniture attractively. Before you take photos of your furniture, make sure that it's staged attractively. This means arranging the furniture in a way that shows off its best features. You may also want to add some decorative pillows, throws, or plants to help stage the furniture.

Take multiple photos from different angles. Don't just take one photo of each piece of furniture. Take multiple photos from different angles so that potential buyers can get a good look at the furniture from all sides. Be sure to take close-up photos of any damage or imperfections so that potential buyers know exactly what they're getting.

By following these tips, you can take high-quality photos of your furniture that will help you to sell it quickly and for a fair price.

Write detailed descriptions

In addition to taking high-quality photos, you also need to write detailed descriptions of your furniture. Your descriptions should be informative and engaging, and they should highlight the best features of your furniture. Here are some tips for writing detailed descriptions of your furniture:

Start with a strong headline. Your headline is the first thing that potential buyers will see, so make sure that it's attention-grabbing and informative. For example, instead of writing "Sofa for sale," you could write "Comfy and stylish sofa perfect for relaxing." This gives potential buyers a reason to read the rest of your description.

Include key details. In the body of your description, be sure to include key details about your furniture, such as the dimensions, the materials, and the condition. You should also mention any special features or unique details about the furniture. For example, if your sofa has a reclining feature, be sure to mention that in the description.

Use descriptive language. When describing your furniture, use descriptive language to help potential buyers visualize it. For example, instead of writing "This sofa is comfortable," you could write "This sofa is upholstered in soft, plush velvet and has deep, cushy cushions that you'll sink into." This gives potential buyers a better idea of what the sofa is like to sit on.

Be honest about the condition of your furniture. If your furniture has any damage or imperfections, be honest about it in your description. Don't try to hide or downplay the damage, as this will only disappoint potential buyers when they see the furniture in person. Instead, be upfront about the damage and offer to provide additional photos or videos if needed.

By following these tips, you can write detailed descriptions of your furniture that will help you to sell it quickly and for a fair price.

Market furniture effectively

Once you've taken high-quality photos and written detailed descriptions of your furniture, you need to market it effectively to reach potential buyers. Here are some tips for marketing your furniture effectively:

List your furniture on online marketplaces. There are many online marketplaces where you can sell your furniture, such as eBay, Craigslist, and Facebook Marketplace. These marketplaces have a large audience of potential buyers, so you're more likely to find a buyer for your furniture quickly. When listing your furniture on an online marketplace, be sure to include high-quality photos, detailed descriptions, and a competitive price.

Promote your furniture on social media. Social media is a great way to promote your furniture to potential buyers. Share photos of your furniture on your social media pages and encourage your friends and followers to share the posts with their friends. You can also join local furniture selling groups on social media and post your furniture there.

Run a garage sale. If you're looking to sell your furniture quickly, you can run a garage sale. Garage sales are a great way to get rid of a lot of furniture in a short amount of time. When running a garage sale, be sure to advertise it well in advance and price your furniture competitively.

Consign your furniture to a consignment shop. If you don't have the time or energy to sell your furniture yourself, you can consign it to a consignment shop. Consignment shops sell furniture on behalf of their clients, and they typically take a percentage of the sale price as their commission. Consigning your furniture to a consignment shop is a good option if you're looking for a hassle-free way to sell your furniture.

By following these tips, you can market your furniture effectively and increase your chances of selling it quickly and for a fair price.

Negotiate with buyers

When selling furniture, you may need to negotiate with buyers to get the best possible price. Here are some tips for negotiating with buyers:

Be prepared to negotiate. Before you start negotiating with a buyer, be prepared to give and take. This means being willing to lower your price slightly if the buyer is willing to pay more than your asking price. It also means being prepared to walk away from the deal if the buyer is not willing to meet your price.

Start with a fair price. When pricing your furniture, be sure to set a fair price that is in line with the market value of similar items. This will give you some wiggle room to negotiate with buyers.

Be confident and friendly. When negotiating with buyers, be confident and friendly. This will make the negotiation process more pleasant and will increase your chances of reaching a deal. Be willing to answer any questions that the buyer has and be honest about the condition of your furniture.

Be willing to compromise. In most cases, you will need to compromise in order to reach a deal with a buyer. This means being willing to lower your price slightly or to accept a payment plan. Be flexible and willing to work with the buyer to find a solution that works for both of you.

By following these tips, you can negotiate with buyers effectively and increase your chances of selling your furniture quickly and for a fair price.
