Durable Materials

Kensington garden furniture is crafted from durable materials that are built to withstand the elements and heavy use, ensuring that your investment will last for many years to come.

Teak: Known for its exceptional durability and natural beauty, teak is a premium hardwood that is resistant to rot, decay, and insects. It is often used for outdoor furniture due to its ability to withstand harsh weather conditions and its beautiful grain pattern.

Aluminum: Aluminum is a lightweight and durable metal that is resistant to rust and corrosion. It is often used for outdoor furniture frames and is particularly well-suited for coastal areas or other environments with high humidity.

Stainless Steel: Stainless steel is an alloy that is highly resistant to rust and corrosion. It is often used for outdoor furniture hardware and accessories, such as screws, bolts, and brackets.

All-Weather Fabrics: Kensington uses high-quality, all-weather fabrics that are resistant to fading, moisture, and mildew. These fabrics are designed to withstand the elements and retain their beauty over time.

By choosing durable materials, Kensington ensures that its garden furniture is not only stylish but also built to last, providing you with years of enjoyment and value.

FAQ

If you have questions about choosing and caring for garden furniture, here are some frequently asked questions that might help:

Question 1: What materials are best for garden furniture?
Answer 1: Teak, aluminum, and stainless steel are all durable materials that can withstand the elements and heavy use. Wicker and resin are also popular choices, offering a combination of style and affordability.

Question 2: How do I protect my garden furniture from the sun and rain?
Answer 2: Use furniture covers when the furniture is not in use. Choose covers made of waterproof and UV-resistant materials to provide maximum protection from the elements.

Question 3: How do I clean my garden furniture?
Answer 3: The best cleaning method depends on the material. For teak furniture, use a teak cleaner and protector. For aluminum furniture, use a mild soap and water solution. For wicker furniture, vacuum or use a soft brush to remove dirt and debris.

Question 4: How often should I maintain my garden furniture?
Answer 4: Regular maintenance will help extend the life of your garden furniture. Inspect your furniture monthly for any loose screws or bolts and tighten them as needed. Clean your furniture every few months or as needed, depending on the material and environment.

Question 5: Can I leave my garden furniture outside all year round?
Answer 5: Some furniture is designed to be left outdoors year-round, while others may need to be stored indoors during harsh weather conditions. Check the manufacturer's recommendations for specific care instructions.

Question 6: Where can I buy high-quality garden furniture?
Answer 6: You can find a wide selection of high-quality garden furniture at reputable home and garden stores, both online and offline. Look for brands known for their craftsmanship and durability.

By following these tips, you can choose, maintain, and enjoy your garden furniture for many years to come.

Tips

Here are a few practical tips to help you choose, care for, and enjoy your garden furniture:

Tip 1: Choose the right materials for your needs. Consider the climate in your area, how often you will use the furniture, and your personal style. Teak, aluminum, and stainless steel are all durable and weather-resistant materials, while wicker and resin offer a more budget-friendly option.

Tip 2: Measure your space before you buy. Make sure you have enough room for the furniture you want, plus some extra space for movement and accessories. It's also a good idea to consider the scale of the furniture in relation to the size of your outdoor space.

Tip 3: Protect your furniture from the elements. Use furniture covers when the furniture is not in use. Choose covers made of waterproof and UV-resistant materials to provide maximum protection from the sun, rain, and wind.

Tip 4: Clean your furniture regularly. The best cleaning method depends on the material. For teak furniture, use a teak cleaner and protector. For aluminum furniture, use a mild soap and water solution. For wicker furniture, vacuum or use a soft brush to remove dirt and debris.

By following these tips, you can choose, maintain, and enjoy your garden furniture for many years to come.
