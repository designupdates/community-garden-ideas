Question 1: What materials are commonly used for garden furniture?

Answer: Garden furniture is typically crafted from various materials, each with unique properties and characteristics. Popular options include:

  • Wood: Wood offers a classic and timeless look, with options like teak, cedar, and acacia known for their durability and resistance to outdoor elements.
  • Metal: Metal furniture, often made from aluminum or wrought iron, provides a sleek and contemporary aesthetic. It is known for its strength and low maintenance requirements.
  • Plastic: Plastic furniture is lightweight, affordable, and comes in a wide range of colors and designs. It is a popular choice for casual outdoor settings.
  • Wicker and Rattan: Wicker and rattan furniture adds a touch of natural charm to your garden. These materials are known for their flexibility and ability to withstand various weather conditions.

Question 6: How can I protect my garden furniture from harsh weather?

Answer: To safeguard your garden furniture from harsh weather conditions, consider these tips:

  • Apply protective coatings or sealants to wood and metal furniture to prevent rust and decay.
  • Store cushions and pillows indoors or use weather-resistant covers when not in use.
  • Choose furniture made from durable materials that can withstand outdoor elements, such as teak or aluminum.
  • Avoid placing furniture in direct sunlight for extended periods to prevent fading and damage.
