Art Deco Style Furniture: A Guide to the Glamorous Era

Written by Luffy Oct 31, 2028 Â· 7 min read

Art Deco style furniture exudes the glamour and elegance of the 1920s and 1930s. With its bold geometric lines, rich materials, and luxurious details, Art Deco furniture adds a touch of sophistication and style to any interior.

This style evolved during a period of great technological and social change, reflecting the optimism and modernity of the era. Art Deco furniture often incorporates elements from ancient Egypt, the Far East, and Cubism, creating a truly eclectic and captivating look.

In this article, we will delve into the key characteristics of Art Deco style furniture, explore the materials and techniques used, and provide tips on how to incorporate this iconic style into your own home.

art deco style furniture

Art Deco style furniture is characterized by its bold geometric lines, rich materials, and luxurious details. Its key features include:

  • Geometric shapes
  • Bold lines
  • Rich colors
  • Luxury materials
  • Exotic influences
  • Streamlined forms
  • Functionality

Art Deco furniture often incorporates elements from ancient Egypt, the Far East, and Cubism, creating a truly eclectic and captivating look.

Geometric shapes

Geometric shapes are one of the most defining characteristics of Art Deco style furniture. These shapes are often bold and exaggerated, creating a sense of movement and dynamism.

  • Circles and semicircles

    Circles and semicircles are commonly used in Art Deco furniture, both as decorative elements and as structural components. They can be found in everything from chair backs to table legs.

  • Squares and rectangles

    Squares and rectangles are another common geometric shape in Art Deco furniture. They are often used to create a sense of order and balance, and can be found in everything from cabinet doors to headboards.

  • Triangles and zigzags

    Triangles and zigzags are often used in Art Deco furniture to create a sense of excitement and energy. They can be found in everything from lampshades to upholstery patterns.

  • Streamlined forms

    Streamlined forms are another important geometric shape in Art Deco furniture. These forms are designed to minimize drag and create a sense of movement. They can be found in everything from car dashboards to furniture legs.

The use of geometric shapes in Art Deco furniture helps to create a sense of glamour and sophistication. These shapes are often combined with rich colors and materials to create a truly unique and eye-catching style.

Bold lines

Bold lines are another defining characteristic of Art Deco style furniture. These lines are often used to create a sense of movement and energy, and can be found in everything from furniture frames to decorative details.

  • Vertical lines

    Vertical lines are often used in Art Deco furniture to create a sense of height and grandeur. They can be found in everything from skyscrapers to armoires.

  • Horizontal lines

    Horizontal lines are often used in Art Deco furniture to create a sense of balance and order. They can be found in everything from tables to headboards.

  • Diagonal lines

    Diagonal lines are often used in Art Deco furniture to create a sense of movement and excitement. They can be found in everything from chair legs to lampshades.

  • Curved lines

    Curved lines are often used in Art Deco furniture to create a sense of softness and elegance. They can be found in everything from sofas to mirrors.

The use of bold lines in Art Deco furniture helps to create a sense of glamour and sophistication. These lines are often combined with geometric shapes and rich colors to create a truly unique and eye-catching style.

Rich colors

Rich colors are another defining characteristic of Art Deco style furniture. These colors are often bold and saturated, and can be found in everything from upholstery to paint.

  • Red

    Red is a popular color in Art Deco furniture, as it is associated with luxury and glamour. It can be found in everything from velvet sofas to lacquered cabinets.

  • Black

    Black is another popular color in Art Deco furniture, as it is associated with sophistication and elegance. It can be found in everything from leather chairs to ebony tables.

  • Gold

    Gold is often used in Art Deco furniture to add a touch of opulence. It can be found in everything from gilded mirrors to brass hardware.

  • Green

    Green is a popular color in Art Deco furniture, as it is associated with nature and growth. It can be found in everything from upholstered chairs to painted walls.

The use of rich colors in Art Deco furniture helps to create a sense of glamour and sophistication. These colors are often combined with bold lines and geometric shapes to create a truly unique and eye-catching style.

Luxury materials

Art Deco style furniture is often made from luxury materials, such as exotic woods, leather, and marble. These materials add a touch of glamour and sophistication to any piece of furniture.

Exotic woods, such as ebony, rosewood, and mahogany, are popular choices for Art Deco furniture. These woods have a beautiful grain and rich color, and they are often used to create stunning pieces of furniture.

Leather is another popular material for Art Deco furniture. Leather is durable and easy to clean, and it can be used to create a variety of different looks. It can be used to upholster chairs and sofas, or it can be used to create decorative details, such as trim and piping.

Marble is a classic choice for Art Deco furniture. Marble is a beautiful and durable material, and it can be used to create a variety of different looks. It can be used to create tabletops, countertops, and even entire pieces of furniture, such as coffee tables and side tables.

The use of luxury materials in Art Deco furniture helps to create a sense of glamour and sophistication. These materials are often combined with bold lines and geometric shapes to create a truly unique and eye-catching style.

Exotic influences

Art Deco style furniture often incorporates exotic influences from around the world. These influences can be seen in the use of materials, colors, and motifs.

One of the most common exotic influences in Art Deco furniture is the use of Egyptian motifs. These motifs can be found in everything from furniture legs to decorative details. Egyptian motifs are often associated with luxury and glamour, and they add a touch of exoticism to any piece of furniture.

Another common exotic influence in Art Deco furniture is the use of Asian motifs. These motifs can be found in everything from furniture shapes to decorative details. Asian motifs are often associated with nature and spirituality, and they add a touch of serenity to any piece of furniture.

Art Deco furniture also incorporates influences from other cultures, such as African, Aztec, and Art Nouveau. These influences can be seen in the use of materials, colors, and motifs. The eclectic mix of influences in Art Deco furniture creates a truly unique and eye-catching style.

The use of exotic influences in Art Deco furniture helps to create a sense of glamour and sophistication. These influences add a touch of the unknown and the exotic to any piece of furniture, and they help to create a truly unique and eye-catching style.

Streamlined forms

Streamlined forms are another defining characteristic of Art Deco style furniture. These forms are designed to minimize drag and create a sense of movement. They can be found in everything from car dashboards to furniture legs.

  • Curved edges

    Curved edges are a common feature of streamlined forms. These edges help to create a sense of movement and fluidity. They can be found in everything from tabletops to chair backs.

  • Rounded corners

    Rounded corners are another common feature of streamlined forms. These corners help to create a softer and more inviting look. They can be found in everything from cabinets to mirrors.

  • Tapered legs

    Tapered legs are often used in streamlined forms. These legs help to create a sense of lightness and movement. They can be found in everything from chairs to tables.

  • Asymmetrical shapes

    Asymmetrical shapes are sometimes used in streamlined forms. These shapes help to create a sense of excitement and energy. They can be found in everything from lampshades to furniture frames.

The use of streamlined forms in Art Deco furniture helps to create a sense of glamour and sophistication. These forms are often combined with bold lines and geometric shapes to create a truly unique and eye-catching style.
