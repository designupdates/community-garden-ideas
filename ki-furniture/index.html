Traditional joinery

Traditional joinery is a key part of ki furniture making. Ki furniture makers use traditional joinery techniques to create furniture that is strong, durable, and beautiful.

One of the most common traditional joinery techniques used in ki furniture making is the mortise and tenon joint. This joint is created by cutting a mortise (a square or rectangular hole) in one piece of wood and a tenon (a protruding tongue) on another piece of wood. The tenon is then inserted into the mortise and secured with glue or pegs.

Another common traditional joinery technique used in ki furniture making is the dovetail joint. This joint is created by cutting a series of interlocking pins and tails on two pieces of wood. The pins and tails are then fitted together to create a strong and durable joint.

Traditional joinery techniques are also used to create decorative elements on ki furniture. For example, ki furniture makers may use dovetail joints to create intricate patterns on the fronts of drawers or doors.

Traditional joinery is a complex and skilled craft. Ki furniture makers who use traditional joinery techniques take great pride in their work. They know that the furniture they create will last for generations.
