Check the condition carefully. G Plan furniture is built to last, but it's still important to check for any signs of damage or wear and tear.

When checking the condition of G Plan furniture second hand, there are a few things you should look for:

  • Scratches and dents. These are the most common signs of damage on G Plan furniture. While small scratches and dents can be easily repaired, larger ones may be more difficult to fix.
  • Fading. G Plan furniture is typically made from teak or walnut, which can fade over time. However, excessive fading can be a sign that the furniture has been exposed to too much sunlight or heat.
  • Warping. Warping can occur if the furniture has been exposed to moisture or extreme temperatures. It can be difficult to repair, so it's important to check for any signs of warping before you buy.
  • Loose joints. Loose joints can be a sign that the furniture has been damaged or poorly constructed. They can also be a tripping hazard, so it's important to tighten any loose joints before using the furniture.
  • Upholstery damage. If the furniture is upholstered, check for any stains, tears, or rips. Upholstery damage can be difficult to repair, so it's important to factor in the cost of repairs when considering buying a piece of upholstered G Plan furniture.

Once you've checked for any signs of damage or wear and tear, you can decide whether or not the furniture is right for you. If you're not sure about something, it's always a good idea to get a second opinion from a professional.
