Are you looking for a way to get rid of your old furniture without adding to the landfill? The Stevenage Furniture Recycling Scheme is a great way to do just that. The scheme is a collaboration between Stevenage Borough Council and several local charities, and it provides a free service to residents of Stevenage who want to dispose of their unwanted furniture.

The scheme is open to all types of furniture, including beds, chairs, tables, sofas, and wardrobes. The only items that are not accepted are items that are broken or damaged beyond repair, or items that are contaminated with hazardous materials.

To use the scheme, simply call the council on 01438 242266 or email furniture.re-use@stevenage.gov.uk to arrange a collection. The council will then collect your furniture from your home and take it to one of the participating charities. The charities will then either refurbish the furniture and sell it on, or they will recycle it into new products.

Furniture is either refurbished or recycled

Once your furniture has been collected by the Stevenage Furniture Recycling Scheme, it will be taken to one of the participating charities. The charities will then assess the furniture to see if it can be refurbished. If the furniture is in good condition, the charities will repair and clean it, and then sell it on in their shops.

If the furniture is not in good enough condition to be refurbished, the charities will recycle it. The furniture will be broken down into its component parts, and the materials will be used to create new products.

By refurbishing and recycling furniture, the Stevenage Furniture Recycling Scheme helps to reduce landfill waste and supports local charities.

Reduces landfill waste

Landfill waste is a major environmental problem. When organic materials, such as furniture, are sent to landfill, they decompose and produce methane gas. Methane is a greenhouse gas that is 25 times more potent than carbon dioxide. It is also a major contributor to climate change.

The Stevenage Furniture Recycling Scheme helps to reduce landfill waste by diverting furniture from landfill and recycling it into new products. In 2021, the scheme diverted over 100 tonnes of furniture from landfill. This is equivalent to the weight of approximately 100 cars.

By reducing landfill waste, the Stevenage Furniture Recycling Scheme helps to protect the environment and reduce climate change.

Here are some additional benefits of reducing landfill waste:

  • Conserves natural resources

    When furniture is recycled, it can be used to create new products, which reduces the need to extract and process raw materials.

  • Saves energy

    Recycling furniture requires less energy than manufacturing new furniture from raw materials.

  • Reduces pollution

Tips

Here are four practical tips to help you keep your garden furniture looking its best:

Tip 1: Clean your garden furniture regularly.
Dirt and grime can build up on your garden furniture over time, making it look dull and tired. To prevent this, clean your garden furniture regularly with a mild soap solution and a soft cloth. Be sure to rinse the furniture thoroughly with clean water afterwards.

Tip 2: Protect your garden furniture from the elements.
When not in use, store your garden furniture in a dry place to protect it from the elements. If you don't have a storage space, cover your garden furniture with a waterproof cover.

Tip 3: Treat your garden furniture regularly.
To protect your garden furniture from the sun's UV rays and other damage, treat it with a suitable sealant or protector. Be sure to follow the manufacturer's instructions carefully.

Tip 4: Repair your garden furniture promptly.
If your garden furniture gets damaged, repair it promptly to prevent further damage. For example, if a screw comes loose, tighten it immediately. If a piece of wood cracks, glue it back together.

By following these tips, you can keep your garden furniture looking its best for many years to come.
