Hardwoods, such as oak and madogany, are known for their strength and durability, while softwoods, such as pine and fir, are more prone to dents and scratches.
  • Hardwoods: Known for their strength, hardness, and durability. They are less prone to denting and scratching, making them ideal for furniture pieces that will be subjected to heavy use, such as dining tables, chairs, and bed frames.
  • Softwoods: Less durable than hardwoods, but more lightweight and easier to work with. They are more prone to denting and scratching, so they may not be the best choice for furniture pieces that will receive a lot of wear and tear. However, they are often used for furniture pieces that are not subjected to heavy use, such as dressers, nightstands, and bookcases.
  • Veneers: Thin slices of hardwood that are glued to a less expensive core material, such as plywood. Veneers provide the look and feel of hardwood at a lower cost, but they are not as durable as solid hardwood. They are often used for furniture pieces that are not subjected to heavy use, such as cabinets and tables.
  • Engineered wood: A type of wood product that is made from wood fibers or strands that are bonded together with resin. Engineered wood is more durable and dimensionally stable than traditional solid wood, and it is less likely to warp or crack. It is often used for furniture pieces that are subjected to heavy use, such as kitchen cabinets and countertops.
When choosing furniture timber, it is important to consider the intended use of the piece and the amount of wear and tear it is likely to receive. Some species are harvested from sustainably managed forests, ensuring that they are replenished for future generations. Sustainability refers to the practice of harvesting timber in a way that does not damage the environment or deplete the resource for future generations. Sustainable forestry practices include planting new trees to replace those that are harvested, protecting biodiversity, and minimizing the use of chemicals and fertilizers. Answer 3: When choosing garden furniture, it is important to consider the size of your space. Answer 5: There are many different ways to accessorize your garden furniture. You can add cushions and pillows for comfort, throws and blankets for warmth, and plants and flowers for color. You can also add lighting to create a cozy and inviting atmosphere.

Question 6: How do I store my garden furniture during the winter?
