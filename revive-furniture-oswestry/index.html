French polishing

French polishing is a traditional technique for finishing wood furniture. It involves applying multiple layers of shellac to the wood, and then rubbing it with a pad to create a smooth, glossy finish.

  • Traditional French polishing: This is the most traditional method of French polishing, and it involves using a pad made of natural materials, such as cotton or wool. The pad is dipped in shellac and then rubbed over the wood in a circular motion. This process is repeated multiple times, until the desired finish is achieved.
  • Mechanical French polishing: This method of French polishing uses a machine to apply the shellac to the wood. This is a faster method than traditional French polishing, but it can be more difficult to achieve a smooth, even finish.
  • Spirit polishing: This method of French polishing uses a mixture of shellac and alcohol to create a finish. This finish is less durable than traditional French polishing, but it is also less expensive.
  • Water-based French polishing: This method of French polishing uses a water-based shellac to create a finish. This finish is more durable than spirit polishing, but it is also more expensive.

At Revive Furniture Oswestry, our skilled craftsmen use traditional French polishing techniques to create a beautiful, durable finish on your furniture. We use only the highest quality materials and take the time to ensure that each piece is finished to the highest standard.

Woodturning

Woodturning is the process of using a lathe to create round objects from wood. This can be used to create a variety of items, including furniture, bowls, and vases.

  • Spindle turning: This is the most basic type of woodturning, and it involves creating a cylindrical object from a piece of wood. This can be used to create a variety of items, such as table legs and chair spindles.
  • Faceplate turning: This type of woodturning involves mounting a piece of wood to a faceplate, which is then rotated on the lathe. This can be used to create a variety of items, such as bowls and plates.
  • Hollow turning: This type of woodturning involves creating a hollow object from a piece of wood. This can be used to create a variety of items, such as vases and boxes.
  • Ornamental turning: This type of woodturning involves creating decorative objects from wood. This can be used to create a variety of items, such as finials and carvings.

Veneering

Veneering is the process of applying a thin layer of wood to a substrate, such as plywood or MDF. This can be done to improve the appearance of the substrate, or to create a more durable surface.

There are two main types of veneers: natural veneers and engineered veneers. Natural veneers are made from real wood, while engineered veneers are made from a combination of wood and other materials.

Natural veneers are more expensive than engineered veneers, but they offer a more authentic look and feel. Engineered veneers are more durable and less expensive than natural veneers, but they may not have the same look and feel.

Marquetry

Marquetry is the art of applying thin pieces of wood to a substrate to create a decorative design. This can be used to create a variety of objects, including furniture, picture frames, and jewelry.

  • Traditional marquetry: This type of marquetry uses natural wood veneers to create a design. The veneers are cut into thin pieces and then glued to the substrate. This is a very time-consuming process, but it can produce very beautiful results.
  • Modern marquetry: This type of marquetry uses a variety of materials to create a design, including wood, metal, and plastic. This type of marquetry is often more abstract than traditional marquetry.
  • Intarsia: This is a type of marquetry that uses different types of wood to create a three-dimensional design. This type of marquetry is often used to create scenes or landscapes.
  • Parquetry: This is a type of marquetry that uses small pieces of wood to create a geometric design. This type of marquetry is often used to create floors and walls.
