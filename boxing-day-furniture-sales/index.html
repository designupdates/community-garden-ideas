Research and compare

Once you know what you're looking for and have a budget in mind, it's time to start doing your research. Here are some tips:

  • Compare prices online.

By doing your research and comparing prices, you'll be able to find the best Boxing Day furniture deals and get the most for your money.

Set a budget

Setting a budget before you start shopping is essential for avoiding overspending and staying within your financial means. Here are some tips for setting a budget for Boxing Day sales:

  1. Determine how much you can afford to spend. Consider your current financial situation and your overall financial goals. How much money can you realistically allocate to Boxing Day shopping without putting yourself in a difficult financial position?

By following these tips, you can set a budget for Boxing Day sales that will help you stay within your financial means and get the furniture you need for your home.

Shop early

One of the best ways to get the best deals on Boxing Day furniture sales is to shop early. Here are some reasons why:

  1. The best selection. The earlier you shop, the better the selection of furniture will be. The most popular items will sell out quickly, so if you have your heart set on a particular piece, it's best to shop early to avoid disappointment.
  2. The best prices. Stores often offer their best discounts on the first day of their Boxing Day sales. As the sale goes on, the prices may start to increase, so it's best to shop early to get the best deals.
  3. Avoid the crowds. Boxing Day sales can be incredibly crowded, especially in the early afternoon. If you shop early, you can avoid the worst of the crowds and have a more relaxed shopping experience.

By shopping early on Boxing Day, you can get the best selection, the best prices, and avoid the crowds. So, if you're serious about finding the best deals on furniture, make sure to set your alarm and head to the store early.

Check return policies

Before you buy any furniture on Boxing Day, be sure to check the store's return policy. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Return period. How long do you have to return the furniture if you're not satisfied? Some stores have a generous return policy that allows you to return furniture up to 30 days or even longer. Others have a shorter return period, such as 14 days or even less.
  • Refund or exchange. What are your options if you return the furniture? Some stores offer a full refund, while others only offer an exchange. Be sure to find out the store's policy before you buy.
  • Conditions. Are there any conditions on returns? Some stores may require you to return the furniture in its original packaging or with all of the original tags attached. Others may charge a restocking fee for returned items.
  • Exceptions. Are there any items that are not eligible for return? Some stores may have a policy that excludes certain items from return, such as clearance items or special orders.

By checking the store's return policy before you buy, you can avoid any surprises if you need to return the furniture. You'll also be able to make an informed decision about whether or not the store's return policy is acceptable to you.

Consider delivery costs

When you're shopping for furniture on Boxing Day, it's important to factor in the cost of delivery. Delivery costs can vary significantly from store to store, so it's worth comparing prices before you buy. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  1. Standard delivery. This is the most basic type of delivery, and it typically involves the delivery of your furniture to your doorstep. Standard delivery costs can range from $50 to $150, depending on the size and weight of your furniture and the distance to your home.
  2. White glove delivery. This type of delivery includes more services, such as bringing your furniture into your home, assembling it, and removing the packaging. White glove delivery costs can range from $150 to $300, depending on the size and weight of your furniture and the distance to your home.
  3. Curbside delivery. This is the most affordable type of delivery, and it involves the delivery of your furniture to the curbside of your home. You will be responsible for bringing the furniture into your home yourself. Curbside delivery costs can range from $25 to $75, depending on the size and weight of your furniture and the distance to your home.

If you're not sure how much delivery will cost, be sure to ask the store before you buy. You can also compare delivery costs from different stores online. By factoring in the cost of delivery, you can avoid any surprises when you're ready to have your furniture delivered.

Don't be afraid to negotiate

One of the best ways to save money on Boxing Day furniture sales is to negotiate with the salesperson. Here are a few tips:

  1. Do your research. Before you go to the store, do your research and compare prices from different stores. This will give you a good idea of the going rate for the furniture you're interested in.
  2. Be prepared to walk away. If the salesperson is unwilling to negotiate, be prepared to walk away. There are plenty of other stores that will be happy to sell you furniture at a fair price.
  3. Be polite and respectful. Even if you're negotiating, it's important to be polite and respectful to the salesperson. Remember, they're just doing their job.
  4. Be willing to compromise. You may not be able to get the furniture for the price you want, but you may be able to compromise on the price or on other terms, such as the delivery date.

By following these tips, you can increase your chances of getting a great deal on Boxing Day furniture sales. So don't be afraid to negotiate, and you may be surprised at how much money you can save.
