Durable and long-lasting

Rattan indoor furniture is durable and long-lasting, making it a great investment for your home. Rattan is a natural material that is resistant to moisture, pests, and UV rays. This means that rattan furniture can withstand the elements and will not fade or deteriorate over time.

In addition to its natural durability, rattan furniture is also strong and sturdy. Rattan is a dense material that can support a lot of weight without breaking or bending. This makes rattan furniture a good choice for both indoor and outdoor use.

Here are some tips for caring for your rattan furniture to ensure that it lasts for many years to come:

  • Clean your rattan furniture regularly with a damp cloth and mild soap. Avoid using harsh chemicals or detergents, as these can damage the rattan.
  • If your rattan furniture gets wet, dry it off immediately with a clean cloth. Do not leave rattan furniture wet for long periods of time, as this can cause the rattan to rot.
  • Store your rattan furniture in a dry place when not in use. This will help to prevent the rattan from becoming damaged by moisture or pests.

With proper care, rattan indoor furniture can last for many years. Its durability and long-lasting nature make it a great investment for your home.

  • Cleaning

    To clean rattan furniture, simply wipe it down with a damp cloth and mild soap. Avoid using harsh chemicals or detergents, as these can damage the rattan. If your rattan furniture gets wet, dry it off immediately with a clean cloth. Do not leave rattan furniture wet for long periods of time, as this can cause the rattan to rot.


  • Dusting

    To dust rattan furniture, use a soft, dry cloth. Avoid using feather dusters or compressed air, as these can damage the rattan. Dust your rattan furniture regularly to prevent the accumulation of dust and dirt.


  • Vacuuming

    To vacuum rattan furniture, use a soft brush attachment. Avoid using a vacuum cleaner with a beater bar, as this can damage the rattan. Vacuum your rattan furniture regularly to remove dust and dirt.


  • Storing

    When not in use, store your rattan furniture in a dry place. This will help to prevent the rattan from becoming damaged by moisture or pests. If you live in a humid climate, consider covering your rattan furniture with a plastic tarp or furniture cover.
