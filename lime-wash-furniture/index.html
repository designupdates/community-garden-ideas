Limewash Your Furniture for a Fresh, New Look

Written by Adams Sep 02, 2026 · 15 min read

Limewash is a traditional paint that has been used for centuries to create a beautiful, aged finish on furniture and walls. It is made from slaked lime, which is a mixture of calcium oxide and water. When limewash is applied to wood, it penetrates the surface and forms a bond that is both durable and flexible. This makes it an excellent choice for furniture that is subject to wear and tear.

Limewash is also easy to apply and can be used on both indoor and outdoor furniture. It is available in a variety of colors, so you can choose the perfect shade to match your decor. Limewash can also be tinted with pigments to create custom colors.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about using limewash on garden furniture:

Question 1: Can I use limewash on any type of garden furniture?

Answer: Limewash can be used on a variety of garden furniture materials, including wood, metal, and wicker. However, it is important to note that limewash is not suitable for use on plastic or vinyl furniture.

Question 2: How do I prepare my garden furniture for limewashing?

Answer: To prepare your garden furniture for limewashing, you should first clean it thoroughly to remove any dirt or debris. If the furniture is made of wood, you may need to sand it lightly to create a smooth surface. Once the furniture is clean and dry, you can apply a coat of primer to help the limewash adhere better.

Question 3: How many coats of limewash do I need to apply?

Answer: The number of coats of limewash you need to apply will depend on the desired finish. For a light, translucent finish, one coat may be sufficient. For a more opaque finish, you may need to apply two or three coats.

Question 4: How long does it take for limewash to dry?

Answer: Limewash typically dries to the touch in about 2-4 hours. However, it is important to allow the limewash to cure completely before using the furniture. This can take up to 2-3 weeks.

Question 5: How do I care for limewashed garden furniture?

Answer: To care for limewashed garden furniture, you should simply wipe it down with a damp cloth as needed. You can also apply a coat of sealant to help protect the finish.

Question 6: Where can I buy limewash?

Question 7: Can I use limewash to create a specific color?
