Eco-Friendly: Mother of Pearl Is a Natural Material That Is Eco-Friendly and Sustainable

Mother of pearl is a natural material that is eco-friendly and sustainable. This is because mother of pearl is a byproduct of the pearl industry. Pearls are formed when a foreign object, such as a piece of sand or grit, becomes lodged in the mantle of an oyster. The oyster then secretes a layer of nacre, or mother of pearl, around the object to protect itself. Over time, the layers of nacre build up and form a pearl.

Once a pearl is harvested, the oyster is often discarded. However, the mother of pearl that lines the oyster's mantle can be used to create beautiful and unique pieces of furniture. Mother of pearl inlay furniture is a sustainable choice because it uses a byproduct of the pearl industry.
