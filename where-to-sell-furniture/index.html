Where to Sell Furniture: A Complete Guide

Written by Christine Jul 25, 2025 Â· 17 min read

Are you looking to declutter your home and make some extra cash? Selling furniture is a great way to do both. But with so many options available, it can be tough to know where to start. In this comprehensive guide, we'll walk you through everything you need to know about selling furniture, from choosing the right platform to pricing your items competitively.

Whether you're selling a single piece of furniture or an entire houseful, there are a few things you'll need to do to get started. First, you'll need to decide where you want to sell your furniture. There are a variety of options available, including online marketplaces, consignment shops, and garage sales. Once you've chosen a selling platform, you'll need to price your items competitively. To do this, you'll need to research the market value of similar items and set your prices accordingly.

Once you've priced your items, you'll need to take some high-quality photos. These photos will be used to promote your furniture online, so it's important to make sure they're clear and well-lit. You'll also need to write detailed descriptions of your items, including the condition, dimensions, and any other relevant information.


Now that you're ready to sell your furniture, let's talk about some of the different selling platforms available. Online marketplaces like eBay and Craigslist are a great option for selling furniture to a wide audience. However, it's important to be aware of the fees associated with selling on these platforms. Consignment shops are another option for selling furniture. With consignment shops, you'll pay a commission to the shop for selling your items.

Where to Sell Furniture

Online marketplaces

Online marketplaces are a great option for selling furniture to a wide audience. With just a few clicks, you can list your items for sale and reach potential buyers from all over the country.

  • eBay:

    eBay is one of the largest online marketplaces in the world, with millions of active users. It's a great place to sell furniture, especially if you're looking to reach a large audience. However, eBay does charge fees for selling items, so be sure to factor those into your pricing.

  • Craigslist:

    Craigslist is a popular classifieds website where you can list items for sale for free. It's a great option for selling furniture locally. However, Craigslist can be a bit more challenging to use than some other online marketplaces, and there's always the risk of scams.

  • Facebook Marketplace:

    Facebook Marketplace is a great option for selling furniture to your friends and family. It's also a good way to reach people in your local area. Facebook Marketplace is free to use, and it's easy to list items for sale.

  • Chairish:

    Chairish is an online marketplace specializing in vintage and antique furniture. It's a great option for selling high-end furniture or unique pieces. Chairish does charge fees for selling items, but they also offer a variety of services to help sellers, such as professional photography and shipping.

When choosing an online marketplace to sell your furniture, consider factors such as fees, convenience, and target audience. Some marketplaces are better suited for selling certain types of furniture than others. For example, eBay is a good option for selling mass-market furniture, while Chairish is a better option for selling high-end or unique pieces.

Consignment shops

Consignment shops are another great option for selling furniture. With consignment shops, you'll pay a commission to the shop for selling your items. However, this can be a good option if you don't want to deal with the hassle of selling your furniture yourself.

  • Research consignment shops in your area:

    Before you choose a consignment shop, take some time to research the different options in your area. Read online reviews and talk to friends and family to get recommendations. You'll want to choose a consignment shop that specializes in the type of furniture you're selling and that has a good reputation for customer service.

  • Prepare your furniture for consignment:

    Once you've chosen a consignment shop, you'll need to prepare your furniture for sale. This may include cleaning, repairing, or staging your furniture. The better condition your furniture is in, the more likely it is to sell quickly.

  • Set a price for your furniture:

    When you consign your furniture, you'll need to set a price for each item. The consignment shop will typically take a percentage of the sale price as their commission. Be sure to price your furniture competitively so that it sells quickly.

  • Be patient:

    It may take some time for your furniture to sell in a consignment shop. Be patient and don't be afraid to lower your prices if your furniture isn't selling.

Consignment shops can be a great option for selling furniture if you don't want to deal with the hassle of selling it yourself. However, it's important to choose a reputable consignment shop and to be patient while your furniture is being sold.

Garage sales

Garage sales are a great way to sell furniture locally and make some extra cash. To have a successful garage sale, you'll need to plan ahead and prepare your furniture for sale.

Here are some tips for having a successful garage sale:

  • Choose a good location:
    Choose a location for your garage sale that is easy for people to find and access. If you live in a neighborhood, you may want to consider having a multi-family garage sale to attract more shoppers.
  • Advertise your garage sale:
    Once you've chosen a location and date for your garage sale, you'll need to advertise it. You can do this by posting signs in your neighborhood, advertising in local newspapers, or listing your garage sale on websites like Craigslist or GarageSaleFinder.com.
  • Prepare your furniture for sale:
    Before your garage sale, take some time to clean, repair, and stage your furniture. The better condition your furniture is in, the more likely it is to sell. You may also want to consider pricing your furniture competitively to attract buyers.
  • Set up your garage sale:
    On the day of your garage sale, set up your furniture and other items for sale in a neat and organized way. Make sure that your furniture is easy for shoppers to see and access. You may also want to set up a table or chairs where shoppers can sit down and rest.

Garage sales can be a great way to sell furniture and make some extra cash. However, it's important to plan ahead and prepare your furniture for sale in order to have a successful garage sale.

Thrift stores

Thrift stores are another option for selling furniture. Thrift stores typically sell donated items, so you won't get as much money for your furniture as you would if you sold it online or in a consignment shop. However, thrift stores can be a good option if you want to get rid of your furniture quickly and easily.

  • Research thrift stores in your area:

    Before you donate your furniture to a thrift store, take some time to research the different options in your area. Some thrift stores specialize in selling furniture, while others may only sell clothing and other household items. You'll want to choose a thrift store that is likely to sell your furniture quickly.

  • Prepare your furniture for donation:

    Before you donate your furniture to a thrift store, you'll need to prepare it for donation. This may include cleaning, repairing, or staging your furniture. The better condition your furniture is in, the more likely it is to sell quickly.

  • Donate your furniture to the thrift store:

    Once you've chosen a thrift store to donate your furniture to, you'll need to drop it off at the store. Be sure to get a receipt for your donation, as you may be able to deduct the value of your donation from your taxes.

  • Follow up with the thrift store:

    After you've donated your furniture to a thrift store, you may want to follow up with the store to see if your furniture has sold. You can also ask the store if they have any tips for selling furniture in the future.

Thrift stores can be a good option for selling furniture if you want to get rid of your furniture quickly and easily. However, it's important to choose a reputable thrift store and to prepare your furniture for donation before you drop it off at the store.

Auctions

Auctions are another option for selling furniture. Auctions can be a good way to get a fair price for your furniture, but they can also be risky. If you're not careful, you may end up selling your furniture for less than it's worth.

Here are some tips for selling furniture at auction:

  • Choose the right auction:
    There are many different types of auctions, so it's important to choose the right one for your furniture. Some auctions specialize in selling furniture, while others sell a variety of items. You'll want to choose an auction that is likely to attract buyers who are interested in your furniture.
  • Prepare your furniture for auction:
    Before you consign your furniture to an auction, you'll need to prepare it for sale. This may include cleaning, repairing, or staging your furniture. The better condition your furniture is in, the more likely it is to sell for a good price.
  • Set a reserve price:
    When you consign your furniture to an auction, you can set a reserve price. This is the minimum price that you're willing to accept for your furniture. If the bidding doesn't reach your reserve price, your furniture will not be sold.
  • Be prepared to pay a commission:
    Most auctions charge a commission on the sale price of your furniture. This commission can vary depending on the auction house. Be sure to factor the commission into your pricing when you set your reserve price.

Auctions can be a good way to sell furniture, but it's important to do your research and choose the right auction for your furniture. You should also be prepared to pay a commission on the sale price of your furniture.

  • Write detailed descriptions of your furniture:

Be sure to include information about the condition, dimensions, and any other relevant details. Be sure to price your furniture slightly below the market value to make it more attractive to potential buyers.

  • Promote your furniture on social media:

You can do this by sharing your listings on your personal page, in relevant groups, and on local buy and sell pages. You can also use social media advertising to reach a wider audience.

Social media can be a great way to sell furniture to your friends, family, and followers. By taking high-quality photos, writing detailed descriptions, pricing your furniture competitively, and promoting your listings on social media, you can increase your chances of selling your furniture quickly and easily.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about selling garden furniture:

Question 1: What is the best way to sell garden furniture?
Answer 1: There are a few different ways to sell garden furniture, including online marketplaces, consignment shops, garage sales, and local classifieds. The best way to sell your garden furniture will depend on your individual needs and circumstances.

Question 2: Where can I sell my garden furniture online?
Answer 2: There are a number of online marketplaces where you can sell your garden furniture, including eBay, Craigslist, and Facebook Marketplace. You can also try selling your garden furniture on specialized websites that sell outdoor furniture.

Question 3: How do I prepare my garden furniture for sale?
Answer 3: Before you sell your garden furniture, you should clean and repair it as needed. You should also take high-quality photos of your furniture and write detailed descriptions of each item.

Question 4: How do I price my garden furniture?
Answer 4: When pricing your garden furniture, you should consider the condition of the furniture, the original purchase price, and the current market value of similar items. You should also factor in any repairs or cleaning that you have done to the furniture.

Question 5: What are some tips for selling garden furniture quickly?
Answer 5: To sell your garden furniture quickly, you should price it competitively, take high-quality photos, and write detailed descriptions. You should also promote your listings on multiple platforms and be willing to negotiate on price.

Question 6: What should I do if my garden furniture doesn't sell?
Answer 6: If your garden furniture doesn't sell, you can try lowering the price, promoting your listings more aggressively, or consigning your furniture to a furniture consignment store.

Question 7: Can I sell garden furniture that is damaged or in poor condition?
Answer 7: You can sell garden furniture that is damaged or in poor condition, but you will need to be transparent about the condition of the furniture and price it accordingly. You may also want to consider repairing or refinishing the furniture before selling it.
