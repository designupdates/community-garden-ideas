Variety of materials

Transfers are made from a variety of materials, including plastic, fabric, and leather. Each material has its own unique set of advantages and disadvantages.

  • Plastic transfers are the most common type of transfer. They are inexpensive, durable, and easy to clean. However, plastic transfers can be bulky and may not be as breathable as other materials.
  • Fabric transfers are a good choice for items that need to breathe, such as clothing and furniture. Fabric transfers are also relatively inexpensive and easy to clean. However, fabric transfers may not be as durable as plastic transfers.
  • Leather transfers are the most expensive type of transfer. They are durable, breathable, and aesthetically pleasing. However, leather transfers can be difficult to clean and may not be suitable for all items.

When choosing a transfer material, it is important to consider the specific needs of the item you want to protect. If you need a durable and inexpensive transfer, plastic is a good choice. Easy to use

Transfers are very easy to use. They can be applied in just a few simple steps:

  • Clean the surface of the furniture

    Before applying the transfer, be sure to clean the surface of the furniture to remove any dust or dirt. This will help to ensure that the transfer adheres properly.

  • Measure and cut the transfer

    Measure the area of the furniture that you want to cover and cut the transfer to size. Be sure to cut the transfer slightly larger than the area that you want to cover, so that you have some overlap.

  • Apply the transfer

    Peel off the backing of the transfer and apply it to the surface of the furniture. Be sure to smooth out the transfer to remove any wrinkles or bubbles.

  • Trim the excess transfer

    Once the transfer is applied, trim any excess material around the edges. This will give the transfer a clean and finished look.
