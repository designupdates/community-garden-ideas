Tips

Here are a few tips to help you choose and care for your garden furniture:

Tip 1: Consider your climate. If you live in a climate with extreme weather conditions, you'll need to choose furniture that is made from durable materials that can withstand the elements. For example, teak is a good choice for outdoor furniture because it is naturally resistant to rot and decay. Resin is another good choice for outdoor furniture because it is UV-resistant and easy to clean.

Tip 2: Measure your space. Before you shop for garden furniture, it's important to measure your space. This will help you choose furniture that is the right size for your patio or deck. You'll also want to consider the traffic flow in your space when choosing furniture. Make sure that you leave enough room for people to move around comfortably.

Tip 3: Choose comfortable furniture. You'll be spending a lot of time relaxing in your garden furniture, so it's important to choose furniture that is comfortable. Look for furniture with cushions that are firm and supportive. You may also want to consider furniture with adjustable backs and armrests.

Tip 4: Protect your furniture. Garden furniture can be expensive, so it's important to protect your investment. When you're not using your furniture, cover it with a tarp or store it in a garage or shed. You should also clean your furniture regularly to remove dirt and debris.
