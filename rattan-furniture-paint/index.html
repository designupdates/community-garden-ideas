Rattan Furniture Paint: A Guide to Choosing and Using

Written by Christine May 18, 2026 · 13 min read

Rattan furniture is a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor use, thanks to its durability, comfort, and versatile style. However, over time, rattan furniture can start to look tired and worn, especially if it is exposed to the elements.

If your rattan furniture is looking a little worse for wear, don't despair! With a little bit of paint, you can easily give it a new lease of life. However, not all paints are created equal, and it is important to choose the right one for the job.

In this article, we will provide you with a comprehensive guide to choosing and using rattan furniture paint. We will cover everything you need to know, from the different types of paint available to the best way to apply it.

Rattan Furniture Paint

Choosing the right rattan furniture paint is essential for a successful paint job. Here are 10 important points to keep in mind:

  • Use water-based paint.
  • Choose a paint that is specifically designed for rattan.
  • Select a paint color that complements your décor.
  • Prepare the rattan surface by cleaning and sanding it.
  • Apply a primer to help the paint adhere better.
  • Use a brush or sprayer to apply the paint.
  • Apply multiple thin coats of paint.
  • Allow the paint to dry completely between coats.
  • Protect the painted finish with a sealant.
  • Follow the manufacturer's instructions carefully.

By following these tips, you can achieve a beautiful and durable paint finish on your rattan furniture.

Use water-based paint.

Water-based paint is the best choice for painting rattan furniture because it is easy to apply, dries quickly, and is less likely to chip or peel. Water-based paint is also more environmentally friendly than oil-based paint, as it contains fewer VOCs (volatile organic compounds).

When choosing a water-based paint for rattan furniture, be sure to select one that is specifically designed for exterior use. Exterior paints are more durable and resistant to fading than interior paints.

To apply water-based paint to rattan furniture, follow these steps:

  1. Clean the rattan furniture with a damp cloth to remove any dirt or dust.
  2. Sand the rattan furniture lightly with fine-grit sandpaper to create a smooth surface for the paint to adhere to.
  3. Apply a coat of primer to the rattan furniture. Primer helps the paint to adhere better and prevents the rattan from absorbing too much paint.
  4. Apply two or three thin coats of water-based paint to the rattan furniture, allowing each coat to dry completely before applying the next.
  5. Once the paint is completely dry, protect the finish with a clear sealant.

By following these steps, you can achieve a beautiful and durable paint finish on your rattan furniture that will last for years to come.

Choose a paint that is specifically designed for rattan.

Not all paints are created equal, and it is important to choose a paint that is specifically designed for rattan furniture. Rattan is a natural material that is susceptible to moisture and UV damage, so it is important to use a paint that will protect it from these elements.

Paints that are specifically designed for rattan furniture are typically made with a water-based acrylic formula. These paints are durable, weather-resistant, and easy to apply. They are also less likely to chip or peel than other types of paint.

When choosing a paint for rattan furniture, be sure to read the label carefully to ensure that it is specifically designed for this type of material. You should also consider the color of the paint and the finish that you want to achieve.

Once you have chosen a paint, be sure to follow the manufacturer's instructions carefully. This will help you to achieve the best possible results.

Select a paint color that complements your décor.

The color of the paint you choose for your rattan furniture will have a big impact on the overall look of your space. It is important to select a color that complements your décor and creates the desired atmosphere.

If you want to create a classic and timeless look, choose a neutral color such as white, black, or gray. These colors will go with any décor and will never go out of style.

If you want to add a pop of color to your space, choose a brighter color such as blue, green, or yellow. These colors will create a more cheerful and inviting atmosphere.

When choosing a paint color, it is also important to consider the size of your space and the amount of natural light that it receives. Dark colors can make a small space feel even smaller, while light colors can make a large space feel more airy and spacious.

Prepare the rattan surface by cleaning and sanding it.

Before you paint your rattan furniture, it is important to prepare the surface by cleaning and sanding it. This will help the paint to adhere better and will result in a smoother, more even finish.

To clean the rattan furniture, use a damp cloth to wipe away any dirt or dust. If the furniture is particularly dirty, you can use a mild soap solution. Be sure to rinse the furniture thoroughly with clean water after cleaning it.

Once the furniture is clean, sand it lightly with fine-grit sandpaper. This will help to smooth the surface and remove any splinters or rough spots. Be sure to dust off the furniture after sanding it.

By following these steps, you will ensure that your rattan furniture is properly prepared for painting.

Apply a primer to help the paint adhere better.

Primer is an essential step in the painting process, as it helps the paint to adhere better to the surface and prevents it from peeling or chipping. When painting rattan furniture, it is especially important to use a primer, as rattan is a smooth material that paint can easily slide off of.

  • Benefits of using a primer:

    Primer helps to create a smooth, even surface for the paint to adhere to. This results in a more durable and professional-looking finish.

  • Types of primers:

    There are many different types of primers available, so be sure to choose one that is specifically designed for rattan furniture. Water-based primers are a good option, as they are easy to apply and clean up.

  • How to apply primer:

    To apply primer, simply use a brush or roller to apply a thin, even coat to the rattan furniture. Be sure to allow the primer to dry completely before painting.

  • Drying time:

    Most primers will dry within a few hours, but it is important to allow the primer to dry completely before painting. This will ensure that the paint adheres properly.

By following these steps, you can ensure that your rattan furniture is properly primed and ready for painting.

Use a brush or sprayer to apply the paint.

Once the primer has dried completely, you can begin applying the paint. You can use either a brush or a sprayer to apply the paint, but a brush will give you more control over the application and will result in a smoother finish.

If you are using a brush, be sure to use a soft-bristled brush that is specifically designed for painting rattan furniture. Apply the paint in thin, even strokes, and be sure to overlap each stroke slightly.

If you are using a sprayer, be sure to follow the manufacturer's instructions carefully. Spray the paint in a light, even coat, and be sure to overlap each stroke slightly.

Allow the first coat of paint to dry completely before applying a second coat. This will help to ensure that the paint adheres properly and results in a durable finish.

Apply multiple thin coats of paint.

It is important to apply multiple thin coats of paint, rather than one thick coat. This will help to ensure that the paint adheres properly and results in a smooth, even finish.

  • Benefits of applying thin coats:

    Applying thin coats of paint allows each coat to dry completely before the next coat is applied. This helps to prevent the paint from peeling or chipping.

  • How to apply thin coats:

    To apply thin coats of paint, use a light touch and avoid overloading the brush or sprayer. Apply the paint in even strokes, and be sure to overlap each stroke slightly.

  • Drying time:

    Allow each coat of paint to dry completely before applying the next coat. This will typically take a few hours, but it is important to allow the paint to dry completely to ensure that it adheres properly.

  • Number of coats:

    The number of coats of paint that you need to apply will depend on the color of the paint and the desired finish. For most colors, two or three coats of paint will be sufficient.

By following these tips, you can ensure that your rattan furniture is painted to perfection.

Protect the painted finish with a sealant.

Once the paint has dried completely, it is important to protect the finish with a sealant. This will help to protect the paint from scratches, fading, and moisture damage.

  • Benefits of using a sealant:

    Sealants help to create a protective barrier over the paint, which makes it more resistant to wear and tear. Sealants also help to protect the paint from fading and moisture damage.

  • Types of sealants:

    There are many different types of sealants available, so be sure to choose one that is specifically designed for rattan furniture. Water-based sealants are a good option, as they are easy to apply and clean up.

  • How to apply a sealant:

    To apply a sealant, simply use a brush or roller to apply a thin, even coat to the painted rattan furniture. Be sure to allow the sealant to dry completely before using the furniture.

  • Drying time:

    Most sealants will dry within a few hours, but it is important to allow the sealant to dry completely before using the furniture. This will ensure that the sealant has had time to cure and create a durable protective barrier.

By following these tips, you can ensure that your rattan furniture is protected from the elements and will last for many years to come.

Follow the manufacturer's instructions carefully.

When painting rattan furniture, it is important to follow the manufacturer's instructions carefully. This will ensure that you are using the correct products and techniques to achieve the best possible results.

  • Read the instructions:

    Before you begin painting, take the time to read the manufacturer's instructions carefully. This will help you to understand the specific requirements for the paint and sealant that you are using.

  • Follow the recommended drying times:

    It is important to allow each coat of paint and sealant to dry completely before applying the next coat. This will ensure that the paint and sealant have had time to cure and create a durable finish.

  • Use the recommended tools and materials:

    The manufacturer's instructions will specify the recommended tools and materials for applying the paint and sealant. Be sure to use the correct tools and materials to ensure the best possible results.

  • Contact the manufacturer if you have any questions:

    If you have any questions about the painting process, do not hesitate to contact the manufacturer. They will be able to provide you with expert advice and support.
