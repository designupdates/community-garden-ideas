Incorporate geometric shapes

Geometric shapes were another popular design element in the 1970s, and they can be used to add a touch of retro flair to your home. Geometric shapes can be found in everything from furniture to wallpaper to textiles.

  • Squares: Squares are a classic geometric shape that can be used to create a variety of different looks. Square furniture, such as coffee tables and ottomans, can add a touch of order and symmetry to a room. Square patterns can also be found on wallpaper, rugs, and textiles.
  • Circles: Circles are another popular geometric shape that was used in 70s style furniture. Circular furniture, such as armchairs and dining tables, can add a touch of softness and femininity to a room. Circular patterns can also be found on wallpaper, rugs, and textiles.
  • Triangles: Triangles are a more angular geometric shape that can be used to add a touch of drama to a room. Triangular furniture, such as sofas and chairs, can create a striking focal point in a room. Triangular patterns can also be found on wallpaper, rugs, and textiles.

When incorporating geometric shapes into your 70s style furniture, don't be afraid to mix and match different shapes and sizes. The more varied the shapes, the more eclectic and interesting your space will look.

Choose natural materials

Natural materials were another popular choice for 70s style furniture. Wood, leather, and wicker were all commonly used in furniture design during this era. Natural materials can add a touch of warmth and organic beauty to your home.

Wood: Wood is a versatile material that can be used to create a variety of different furniture styles. Wooden furniture can be stained, painted, or left natural to showcase the beauty of the wood grain. Wood furniture is also durable and long-lasting, making it a good investment for your home.

Leather: Leather is another durable and stylish material that was popular in 70s style furniture. Leather furniture is available in a variety of colors and styles, so you can find the perfect piece to match your décor. Leather furniture is also easy to care for, making it a good choice for busy families.

Wicker: Wicker is a natural material that is made from the stems of rattan or bamboo plants. Wicker furniture is lightweight and durable, making it a good choice for both indoor and outdoor use. Wicker furniture can also add a touch of bohemian flair to your home.

When choosing natural materials for your 70s style furniture, look for pieces that are well-made and durable. Natural materials can last for many years with proper care, so you can enjoy your furniture for years to come.

Go for oversized pieces

Oversized furniture was another popular trend in the 1970s. Large, comfortable sofas and chairs were perfect for relaxing in and entertaining guests. Oversized furniture can also make a small room feel larger and more inviting.

  • Sofas: Oversized sofas were a staple of 70s living rooms. These sofas were typically modular, meaning that they could be arranged in a variety of different ways to accommodate different seating needs. Oversized sofas are perfect for families and for entertaining guests.
  • Chairs: Oversized chairs were also popular in the 1970s. These chairs were typically large and comfortable, with high backs and deep seats. Oversized chairs are perfect for relaxing in and reading a book or watching a movie.
  • Ottomans: Ottomans are a great way to add extra seating and storage to your living room. Oversized ottomans can be used as footrests, coffee tables, or extra seating. Ottomans are also a great place to store blankets, pillows, and other items.
  • Beds: Oversized beds were also popular in the 1970s. These beds were typically king-size or larger, and they were often equipped with headboards and footboards. Oversized beds are perfect for couples and for people who like to spread out when they sleep.

When choosing oversized furniture, keep in mind the size of your room. You don't want to overwhelm your space with too much furniture. Oversized furniture can also be more expensive than smaller pieces, so be sure to budget accordingly.

If you're looking for a way to add some 70s flair to your home, shag carpet is a great option. It's comfortable, stylish, and easy to care for.

Accessorize with lava lamps

Lava lamps were another popular accessory in the 1970s. These mesmerizing lamps featured a base filled with wax and a metal coil in the center. When the lamp is turned on, the coil heats the wax and causes it to rise and fall, creating a mesmerizing effect.

  • Create a unique and stylish atmosphere: Lava lamps can create a unique and stylish atmosphere in any room. The mesmerizing effect of the lava lamp is sure to draw attention and add a touch of fun to your décor.
  • Provide a soft and relaxing light: Lava lamps provide a soft and relaxing light that is perfect for creating a cozy and inviting atmosphere. The warm glow of the lava lamp can help to reduce stress and promote relaxation.
  • Add a touch of nostalgia: Lava lamps are a great way to add a touch of nostalgia to your home. These lamps were popular in the 1970s and 1980s, and they can help to create a retro look in your home.
  • Affordable and easy to find: Lava lamps are relatively affordable and easy to find. They can be purchased at a variety of stores, including online retailers and home goods stores.

If you're looking for a way to add some fun and style to your home, a lava lamp is a great option. These lamps are affordable, easy to find, and sure to create a unique and stylish atmosphere in any room.
