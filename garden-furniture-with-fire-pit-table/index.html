Safety first: follow guidelines

When using a fire pit table, it's important to follow certain safety guidelines to prevent accidents and injuries.

Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Choose a safe location: Place your fire pit table in a safe location away from overhanging branches, flammable materials, and high-traffic areas.
  • Never leave a fire unattended: Always supervise a fire when it is burning. Never leave it unattended, even for a short period of time.
  • Keep children and pets away from the fire: Teach children and pets to stay away from the fire. Never allow them to play near the fire pit table.
  • Use the fire pit table only for its intended purpose: Do not use the fire pit table for cooking or any other purpose other than burning wood or gas.
  • Never use gasoline or other flammable liquids to start or maintain a fire: Only use approved fire starters or kindling.
  • Extinguish the fire completely before leaving it unattended: Always extinguish the fire completely before leaving it unattended. Use water or a fire extinguisher to extinguish the fire.

By following these safety guidelines, you can help to prevent accidents and injuries and ensure that you and your guests can enjoy your fire pit table safely.

Regular maintenance

To keep your garden furniture and fire pit table looking their best and functioning properly, it's important to perform regular maintenance.

Here are some maintenance tips:

  • Clean your fire pit table regularly: Clean your fire pit table after each use to remove any ash, debris, or grease. Use a damp cloth or sponge and a mild detergent. Rinse the table thoroughly with water and dry it with a clean cloth.
  • Inspect your fire pit table for damage: Regularly inspect your fire pit table for any damage, such as cracks, rust, or loose parts. Repair or replace any damaged parts immediately.
  • Protect your fire pit table from the elements: If you live in an area with harsh weather conditions, protect your fire pit table from the elements by covering it with a tarp or storing it in a garage or shed.
  • Maintain your fire pit table's burner: If your fire pit table has a gas burner, it's important to maintain it properly. Clean the burner regularly and check for any leaks. Have your fire pit table serviced by a qualified technician once a year.

By following these maintenance tips, you can help to extend the life of your garden furniture and fire pit table and keep it looking its best for many years to come.
