The art of Chinese lacquer furniture has a rich history spanning centuries. This ancient practice involves applying layers of lacquer, a natural resin derived from the sap of lacquer trees, to various surfaces such as wood, bamboo, and metal. With its exquisite craftsmanship and durability, Chinese lacquer furniture has become renowned worldwide for its beauty and functionality.

The process of creating lacquerware is meticulous and time-consuming. The lacquer sap is collected, refined, and then mixed with pigments to create different colors and patterns. Multiple layers of lacquer are then applied to the surface and dried in a humid environment, creating a hard and glossy finish that is resistant to water, heat, and insects.

  • Lacquer coffins

  • Furniture

Natural lacquer resin

Lacquer is a natural resin that is derived from the sap of the lacquer tree (Toxicodendron vernicifluum). The lacquer tree is native to China and Japan, and it has been cultivated for thousands of years for its valuable resin.

Lacquer sap is collected by tapping the trunk of the lacquer tree. The sap is then filtered and refined to remove impurities. The resulting lacquer is a viscous liquid that can be applied to various surfaces, such as wood, bamboo, and metal.

Lacquer is a very durable material that is resistant to water, heat, and insects. It also has a beautiful glossy finish that can be polished to a high shine. Lacquerware is often decorated with intricate designs and patterns, which are created by applying multiple layers of lacquer in different colors.

Meticulous craftsmanship
