Tips

Here are some practical tips to help you get the most out of your garden furniture:

1. Choose the right materials: Consider the climate in your area and the intended use of your furniture when selecting materials. For example, aluminum is lightweight and rust-resistant, making it a good choice for outdoor dining sets. Teak is naturally water-resistant, making it ideal for poolside furniture.

2. Protect your furniture from the elements: To extend the life of your garden furniture, protect it from direct sunlight, rain, and snow. Use furniture covers or store your furniture indoors during inclement weather or prolonged periods of non-use.

3. Clean your furniture regularly: Regular cleaning will help remove dirt, debris, and stains from your garden furniture. For aluminum furniture, simply wipe down with a damp cloth. For resin wicker, use a mild soap solution. Teak furniture requires occasional oiling to preserve its natural beauty.

4. Store your cushions properly: To prevent mildew and fading, store your cushions in a dry place when not in use. If your cushions are not water-resistant, use protective covers or store them indoors.
