The Ultimate Guide to Furniture Suede Cleaner: Maintaining the Elegance of your Suede Upholstery

Written by Eveline Apr 15, 2025 Â· 11 min read

Suede furniture adds a touch of sophistication and comfort to any home. However, maintaining its delicate surface requires proper care and a reliable furniture suede cleaner. This comprehensive guide will equip you with the knowledge and techniques to keep your suede upholstery looking pristine and inviting.

Choosing the right furniture suede cleaner is paramount for preserving the fabric's integrity. Natural suede originates from animal hide, making it vulnerable to harsh chemicals and detergents. Opt for specifically formulated suede cleaners that gently remove dirt and stains without damaging the fibers, preserving the fabric's soft texture and vibrant color.

furniture suede cleaner

Here are 10 important points about furniture suede cleaner:

  • Choose a cleaner specifically formulated for suede.
  • Test the cleaner on an inconspicuous area first.
  • Apply the cleaner with a soft cloth or sponge.
  • Work in small sections at a time.
  • Blot up excess cleaner with a clean cloth.
  • Allow the suede to dry completely.
  • Brush the suede with a soft-bristled brush to restore its nap.
  • Protect the suede from future stains with a suede protector.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or detergents.
  • Clean suede furniture regularly to prevent dirt and stains from setting in.

By following these simple tips, you can keep your suede furniture looking its best for years to come.

Choose a cleaner specifically formulated for suede.

Suede is a delicate fabric that requires special care. Using a cleaner that is not specifically formulated for suede can damage the fibers and ruin the appearance of your furniture. Suede cleaners are designed to gently remove dirt and stains without damaging the fabric's nap or color.

  • Look for a cleaner that is pH balanced. pH balanced cleaners will not strip the suede of its natural oils, which can cause the fabric to become dry and brittle.
  • Choose a cleaner that is non-abrasive. Abrasive cleaners can scratch the surface of the suede, damaging the fabric's nap.
  • Opt for a cleaner that is designed for the type of stain you are trying to remove. There are different types of suede cleaners available, each of which is designed to remove specific types of stains. For example, there are cleaners that are specifically designed to remove oil stains, water stains, and ink stains.
  • Test the cleaner on an inconspicuous area first. This will help you to ensure that the cleaner does not damage the suede.

By following these tips, you can choose the best furniture suede cleaner for your needs and keep your suede furniture looking its best.

Work in small sections at a time.

When cleaning your furniture, it is important to work in small sections at a time. This will help to prevent the cleaner from drying out and becoming difficult to remove. It will also help to ensure that you do not miss any spots.

  • Start by cleaning a small, inconspicuous area. This will help you to get the hang of using the cleaner and to make sure that it does not damage the fabric.
  • Once you are comfortable with the cleaner, you can start cleaning larger sections. Work in a systematic manner, cleaning one section at a time.
  • Be sure to overlap your strokes slightly to ensure that you do not miss any spots.
  • As you work, blot up any excess cleaner with a clean cloth. This will help to prevent the cleaner from drying out and becoming difficult to remove.

By following these tips, you can clean your furniture suede cleaner like a pro!

Blot up excess cleaner with a clean cloth.

Once you have applied the cleaner to the suede, it is important to blot up any excess cleaner with a clean cloth. This will help to prevent the cleaner from drying out and becoming difficult to remove. It will also help to prevent the cleaner from leaving a residue on the suede.

To blot up the excess cleaner, use a clean, white cloth. Avoid using colored cloths, as the dye from the cloth could transfer to the suede.

Gently blot the suede with the cloth. Do not rub, as this could damage the fabric.

Continue blotting until the cloth comes away clean. Once all of the excess cleaner has been removed, allow the suede to dry completely.

By following these tips, you can ensure that your suede furniture looks its best.

Allow the suede to dry completely.

Once you have cleaned the suede, it is important to allow it to dry completely before using it again. This will help to prevent the suede from becoming damaged or stained.

To dry the suede, place it in a well-ventilated area away from direct sunlight. You can also use a fan to circulate the air and speed up the drying process.

Do not use a hair dryer to dry the suede, as this could damage the fabric.

Once the suede is dry, you can brush it with a soft-bristled brush to restore its nap. You can also use a suede protector to help prevent future stains.

By following these tips, you can keep your suede furniture looking its best for years to come.

Brush the suede with a soft-bristled brush to restore its nap.

Once the suede is dry, you can brush it with a soft-bristled brush to restore its nap. This will help to remove any dirt or debris that may have become lodged in the fabric.

To brush the suede, use a soft-bristled brush that is specifically designed for suede. Do not use a stiff-bristled brush, as this could damage the fabric.

Gently brush the suede in the direction of the nap. Do not brush against the nap, as this could damage the fabric.

Continue brushing until the nap is restored. Once the nap is restored, you can use a suede protector to help prevent future stains.

By following these tips, you can keep your suede furniture looking its best for years to come.

Protect the suede from future stains with a suede protector.

Once you have cleaned and brushed your suede furniture, it is important to protect it from future stains. You can do this by applying a suede protector.

Suede protectors are available in a variety of forms, including sprays, wipes, and liquids. Choose a suede protector that is specifically designed for the type of suede you have.

To apply a suede protector, follow the instructions on the product label. Be sure to test the protector in an inconspicuous area first to make sure that it does not damage the fabric.

Once the suede protector has been applied, allow it to dry completely. Your suede furniture will now be protected from future stains.

By following these tips, you can keep your suede furniture looking its best for years to come.
