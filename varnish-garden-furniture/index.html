Varnish Your Garden Furniture for a Lasting Shine

Written by Luffy Mar 12, 2026 · 10 min read

As the proud owner of garden furniture, you want to keep it looking its best for as long as possible. Exposure to the elements can take a heavy toll on your furniture, so it's important to protect it with a good quality varnish.

A good varnish will create a barrier between the furniture and the elements, protecting the paint or wood from fading, UV damage, and moisture. Varnish also enhances the look of your wood, bringing out its natural grain and giving it a glossy finish. Choosing the right varnish for your garden furniture is essential to ensure its longevity and continued good looks.

In the following section, we delve into the types of varnish available, the steps involved in applying it, and expert tips for ensuring the best results. With a bit of care and preparation, you can easily extend the lifespan of your garden furniture and enjoy it for many years to come.

Use a good quality varnish

When choosing a varnish for your garden furniture, it's important to choose a good quality product. A good quality varnish will be more durable and will last longer than a cheaper varnish.

  • Protects against the elements: A good quality varnish will create a protective barrier between your furniture and the elements, protecting it from rain, snow, sun, and wind.
  • Resists fading and damage: A good quality varnish will also resist fading and damage from UV rays, ensuring that your furniture stays looking its best for longer.
  • Enhances the appearance of your furniture: A good quality varnish will enhance the natural beauty of your wood furniture, bringing out its grain and giving it a glossy finish.
  • Easy to apply and maintain: A good quality varnish should be easy to apply and maintain. It should be self-levelling and quick-drying, and it should not require frequent reapplication.

By investing in a good quality varnish, you can be sure that your garden furniture will be protected from the elements and will look its best for years to come.

Choose the right type of varnish

There are many different types of varnishes on the market so it's important to choose the right one for your garden furniture. The best type of varnish will depend on the type of wood your furniture is made from and the level of protection you need. Here are a few things to consider when choosing a varnish: * **Type of wood:** Different types of wood require different types of varnish. For example hardwoods like teak and oak can be finished with a penetrating oil or a film-forming varnish. Softwoods like pine and cedar need a film-forming varnish to provide adequate protection. * **Level of protection:** If your furniture is going to be exposed to harsh weather conditions you will need a varnish that provides a high level of protection. Look for a varnish that is UV-resistant and waterproof. * **Finish:** Varnishes come in a variety of finishes including gloss satin and matte. Choose a finish that matches the desired look of your furniture. Once you have considered these factors you can start to narrow down your choices. Here are a few of the most popular types of varnishes for garden furniture: * **Oil-based varnishes:** Oil-based varnishes are very durable and provide a high level of protection. They are also easy to apply and maintain. However oil-based varnishes can take a long time to dry and can yellow over time. * **Water-based varnishes:** Water-based varnishes are less durable than oil-based varnishes but they are easier to clean up and dry more quickly. They are also less likely to yellow over time. * **Polyurethane varnishes:** Polyurethane varnishes are very durable and provide a high level of protection. They are also easy to apply and maintain. However polyurethane varnishes can be expensive. By choosing the right type of varnish for your garden furniture you can be sure that your furniture will be protected from the elements and will look its best for years to come.

Prepare the furniture properly

Before you apply varnish to your garden furniture, it's important to prepare the furniture properly. This will help to ensure that the varnish adheres properly and that the finish is smooth and even.
  • Clean the furniture: The first step is to clean the furniture to remove any dirt, dust, or debris. You can use a mild detergent and water, or a commercial furniture cleaner. Be sure to rinse the furniture thoroughly and allow it to dry completely before proceeding.
  • Sand the furniture: If the furniture is new, you may need to sand it lightly to smooth the surface. Use a fine-grit sandpaper and sand in the direction of the wood grain. Be sure to remove all sanding dust before proceeding.
  • Repair any damage: If the furniture has any damage, such as cracks or dents, you should repair it before applying varnish. You can use a wood filler or a wood glue to repair the damage. Allow the repair to dry completely before proceeding.
  • Remove any hardware: If the furniture has any hardware, such as hinges or handles, you should remove it before applying varnish. This will make it easier to apply the varnish and will prevent the hardware from getting damaged. Once you have prepared the furniture, you are ready to apply the varnish.

    Apply the varnish correctly

    Once you have prepared the furniture, you are ready to apply the varnish. Here are a few tips for applying varnish correctly:
    • Use a good quality brush: A good quality brush will help to apply the varnish evenly and smoothly. Choose a brush that is the right size for the job and that is made from natural bristles.
    • Apply thin coats: It is better to apply several thin coats of varnish than one thick coat. This will help to prevent the varnish from running or sagging.
    • Allow each coat to dry completely: Before applying the next coat of varnish, allow the previous coat to dry completely. This will help to ensure that the varnish adheres properly and that the finish is smooth and even.
    • Sand between coats: If you are applying multiple coats of varnish, you should sand lightly between each coat. This will help to smooth the finish and to remove any dust or debris that may have settled on the varnish. Once you have applied the varnish, allow it to dry completely before using the furniture. This may take several days, depending on the type of varnish you used and the weather conditions.

      Allow the varnish to dry thoroughly

      It is important to allow the varnish to dry thoroughly before using the furniture. This will help to ensure that the varnish has cured properly and that it is durable and long-lasting. Here are a few tips for allowing the varnish to dry thoroughly: - **Allow the varnish to dry in a well-ventilated area:** This will help to speed up the drying process and prevent the varnish from becoming sticky or tacky. - **Do not use the furniture until the varnish is completely dry:** This may take several days, depending on the type of varnish you used and the weather conditions. - **If you need to move the furniture before the varnish is completely dry, be careful not to damage the finish:** You can protect the finish by covering the furniture with a drop cloth or by placing it on a piece of cardboard. Once the varnish is completely dry, you can enjoy your newly varnished garden furniture!

      Maintain the varnish

      To keep your varnished garden furniture looking its best, it is important to maintain the varnish. Here are a few tips for maintaining the varnish: - **Clean the furniture regularly:** Use a mild detergent and water to clean the furniture. Be sure to rinse the furniture thoroughly and allow it to dry completely before using it again. - **Apply a new coat of varnish every few years:** This will help to protect the furniture from the elements and keep it looking its best. - **If the varnish starts to peel or crack, you can repair it by sanding the damaged area and applying a new coat of varnish.** By following these tips, you can keep your varnished garden furniture looking its best for many years to come.
