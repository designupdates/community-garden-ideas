Furniture Retro: A Guide to Decorating with Vintage Pieces

Written by Petter Jun 25, 2029 · 13 min read

Retro furniture is a great way to add a touch of nostalgia to your home. Whether you're looking for a single piece to accentuate your living room or you want to furnish your entire home with vintage finds, there are a few things you should keep in mind.

First, consider the style of your home. Some retro furniture styles, such as mid-century modern or Art Deco, can be paired with almost any type of décor. Others, such as Victorian or Rococo, may look out of place in a modern home. If you're not sure what style of retro furniture will work best in your home, take some time to browse magazines or online retailers to get some ideas.

Once you've chosen a style, it's time to start shopping. You can find retro furniture at a variety of places, including antique stores, thrift stores, and online retailers. When you're shopping, be sure to inspect the pieces carefully for any damage or wear. You should also make sure that the pieces are the right size and scale for your home.

  • Choose a style that complements your home décor.

  • Make sure pieces are the right size and scale for your home.

  • Mix and match different retro styles to create a unique look.

Retro furniture can be a great way to add personality and style to your home. By following these tips, you can find the perfect pieces to create a look that you'll love.

Choosing a Style that Complements Your Home

When choosing retro furniture, it's important to select a style that complements your home's existing décor. If your home has a traditional style, for example, you'll want to choose retro furniture that has a similar look and feel. This could include pieces from the Victorian, Edwardian, or Georgian eras.

If your home has a more modern style, you'll have more flexibility in choosing retro furniture. You could choose pieces from a variety of eras, such as the 1950s, 1960s, or 1970s. You could also mix and match different styles to create a unique look.

It's also important to consider the size and scale of your home when choosing retro furniture. If you have a small home, you'll want to choose pieces that are relatively small and compact. If you have a large home, you can choose pieces that are larger and more imposing.

Finally, don't be afraid to experiment with different colors and patterns when choosing retro furniture. Retro furniture is often available in a wide range of colors and patterns, so you can find pieces that match your personal taste.

By following these tips, you can choose retro furniture that complements your home's existing décor and creates a look that you love.

Inspecting Pieces for Damage or Wear

Once you've chosen a style of retro furniture that you like, it's important to inspect the pieces carefully for any damage or wear. This is especially important if you're buying used furniture. Here are a few things to look for:

  • Scratches and dents: These are common signs of wear and tear on furniture. While small scratches and dents may not be a major concern, larger or deeper ones could be a sign of more serious damage.
  • Tears and holes: These are more serious signs of damage, especially if they're in the fabric or upholstery. Tears and holes can be repaired, but it's important to factor in the cost of repairs before you buy the piece.
  • Loose joints: These can be a sign that the furniture is not well-made or that it has been damaged. Loose joints can be tightened, but it's important to make sure that the furniture is structurally sound before you buy it.
  • Rust and corrosion: These are signs of damage that can be caused by exposure to moisture. Rust and corrosion can be difficult to repair, so it's important to avoid buying furniture that shows signs of these problems.

By inspecting pieces carefully for damage or wear, you can avoid buying furniture that will need to be repaired or replaced soon after you buy it.

Choosing the Right Size and Scale

When choosing retro furniture, it's important to make sure that the pieces are the right size and scale for your home. This means considering the size of the room where the furniture will be placed, as well as the size of the other furniture in the room.

  • Measure the space: Before you start shopping for retro furniture, measure the space where you plan to put it. This will help you determine the maximum size of the furniture that you can accommodate.
  • Consider the other furniture in the room: The size and scale of your retro furniture should also complement the other furniture in the room. For example, if you have a small room, you'll want to choose furniture that is relatively small and compact. If you have a large room, you can choose furniture that is larger and more imposing.
  • Think about the traffic flow: When placing retro furniture in your home, be sure to consider the traffic flow. You don't want to place furniture in a way that blocks walkways or makes it difficult to move around the room.
  • Use rugs to define spaces: Rugs can be used to define different spaces in a room and to create a more cohesive look. For example, you could use a rug to define the seating area in a living room or the dining area in a dining room.

By following these tips, you can choose retro furniture that is the right size and scale for your home and that creates a cohesive and inviting space.

Reupholstering or Refinishing to Update Your Look

If you find a piece of retro furniture that you love but it's not quite the right color or style, don't worry! You can easily update the look of a piece of furniture by reupholstering it or refinishing it.

  • Reupholstering: Reupholstering is the process of replacing the fabric on a piece of furniture. This is a great way to update the look of a piece of furniture and to make it match your existing décor. You can choose from a wide variety of fabrics, including leather, velvet, and cotton.
  • Refinishing: Refinishing is the process of refinishing the wood on a piece of furniture. This can be done to change the color of the wood or to repair any damage. Refinishing can also be used to update the look of a piece of furniture and to make it more modern.
  • Painting: Painting is another great way to update the look of a piece of retro furniture. You can paint the furniture any color you want, and you can even use paint to create unique designs.
  • Adding hardware: Adding new hardware to a piece of retro furniture is a quick and easy way to update its look. You can find a wide variety of hardware at most hardware stores and home improvement stores.

By reupholstering, refinishing, or painting a piece of retro furniture, you can update its look and make it match your existing décor. This is a great way to save money and to create a unique piece of furniture that you'll love for years to come.
