Tips

Here are a few tips to help you choose and care for your garden furniture:

Tip 1: Choose the right material. The material of your garden furniture will determine its durability, appearance, and maintenance requirements. Teak and acacia are both durable hardwoods that are resistant to rot and decay. Aluminum is a lightweight and rust-resistant metal that is perfect for outdoor use. Wicker is a synthetic material that is resistant to fading and UV damage.

Tip 2: Consider your climate. If you live in a climate with extreme weather conditions, you will need to choose furniture that is resistant to fading, UV damage, and rust. You may also want to consider furniture that is easy to clean and maintain.

Tip 3: Choose the right size. Your garden furniture should be proportionate to the size of your outdoor space. You don't want your furniture to be too large or too small. You should also consider the size of your family and how you will be using the furniture.

Tip 4: Protect your furniture. To extend the life of your garden furniture, you should protect it from the elements. Cover your furniture with a tarp or store it in a dry place during the off-season. You should also clean your furniture regularly with a mild soap and water solution.

By following these tips, you can choose and care for garden furniture that will last for years to come.
